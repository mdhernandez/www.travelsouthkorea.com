Gwanghwamun Square

The redesigned Gwanghwamun Square was opened to the public in 2009, right in the heart of Seoul and in the proximity of the famous Gyeongbokgung Palace and the magnificent Bukaksan Mountain. The square has an area of almost 20,000 square meters. The most famous sites in Gwanghwamun Square are the statues of King Sejong the Great and Admiral Yi Sun-sin, but there is so much more to explore!

Sections:

Recovering the History of Gwanghwamun Gate Plaza

Located at the entrance of the plaza and home to the statue of Haetae, a mythical beast that is said to protect palaces from fire.

Reenacting Yukjo Street Plaza

Houses the recreation of Yukjo Street, the main street of old Seoul.

Korea Main Plaza

Where the Statue of King Sejong the Great is located and where Hangeul, the Korean alphabet Sejong created, is displayed on a screen above fountain water.

Civil Participation Urban Culture Plaza

Provides an array of attractions including services for foreign visitors, exhibition halls, and art galleries.

Downtown Plaza

Located near the Statue of Admiral Yi Sun-Sin. Has water based facilities such as fountains and a pond.

Memorial Waterway

Underground water flowing from Gyeongbokgung and Gwanghwamun subway stations to Cheonggyecheon Stream.

Cheonggyecheon Stream Connector

A walkway connecting Cheonggyecheon Stream and Gyeongbokgung Palace

Sunken Square

Connects Gwanghwamun subway station to the square.
