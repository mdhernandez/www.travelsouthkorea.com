Haedong Yonggung Temple

Haedong Yonggung Temple is a Buddhist temple built in 1376, by a teacher named Naong during the Goryeo Dynasty. It is on the east side of Busan and is one of the few temples in Korea that overlooks the sea.

During Buddha's Birthday, celebrations are held at the temple, making the beginning of May a popular time to visit Busan. Many people also visit the temple during the Spring to see the blooming of cherry blossoms, a beautiful sight to behold in Korea. The temple is also a popular spot to visit during New Year's Day when people come to make wishes as they watch the sun rise for the first time in the new year.

Haedong Yonggung Temple operates from 4am to 7pm. There is no admission fee, but there is a fee for using the temple's parking facilities.
