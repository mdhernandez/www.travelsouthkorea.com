War Memorial of Korea

The War Memorial of Korea is based on the location of the former Korean Army headquarters.The memorial not only educates visitors on the Korean War, but also on wars fought earlier in Korea's history. The War Memorial of Korea is open from 9am to 6pm and is open each day of the week except for Mondays. The memorial may also close for national holidays.

Main Sections and Attractions:

Memorial Hall:

This is a place to remember and appreciate the soldiers and policemen of the Republic of Korea who died in various modern conflicts including the Korean War and the Vietnam War. There are also many hallways in this section dedicated to the soldiers of the United Nations who were killed during the Korean War. The names of all these soldiers are inscribed on the stone walls of the Memorial Hall and are listed alphabetically and by nationality.

War History:

This section features war history from the distant past. Military remains and relics are on display dating from the prehistoric era to the Japanese colonial period. This section also features the iconic Turtle Ship and other military technologies that helped Korea fight against Japanese invaders.

Korean War:

In this section visitors can learn about the Korean War from beginning to end. Also featured in this section is ammunition and artifacts used by people involved in both sides of the war. You can find information on how the war started, the major battles of the war, and how the war finally came to an end, as well as stories of civilians displaced by the war.

Expeditionary Forces:

This exhibit depicts the effect of Korean involvement in the Vietnam War and other international conflicts.

ROK Armed Forces:

This section provides the history of Korea's various military branches. Here you can view various documents dating back to the inception of the Republic of Korea's Armed Forces.

Defense Industry:

Here you will find replicas of more modern military technologies. Replicas on display include fighter planes, submarines, destroyers, and modern communication devices.

Large Military Equipment:

Perhaps the most visually striking section of the War Memorial of Korea. Here you can see over 100 pieces of large military equipment, statues, plane models, and boat models.

Other areas:

The War Memorial of Korea also features other attractions such as a screening room, where war documentaries are often screened, and a cultural theater where various displays of culture are performed and viewed by audiences. There is also a large outdoor stadium area where presentations and performances can be seen on certain days.
